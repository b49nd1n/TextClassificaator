 More than 350 000 people have been fined in France for not following the rules during lockdown according to the interior minister Christophe Castaner said hundreds of thousands have been penalised in the first two weeks of the nationwide quarantine during which people can only go outside if it is deemed essential Those breaking the rules risk a fine of 135 which can increase to 200 if caught a second time according to local media Our goal is not to hand out fines Mr Castaner told TV channel La Chaine Info It is to make sure the French stay at home France has been in lockdown since 17 March as authorities work to combat its coronavirus outbreak The lockdown initially planned for two weeks has now been extended until 15 April
